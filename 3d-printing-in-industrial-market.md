<p><ul><li>Stereolithography (SLA)</li><li>Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM)</li><li>Selective Laser Sintering (SLS)</li><li>Direct Metal Laser Sintering (DMLS)</li><li>PolyJet Printing (MJP)</li><li>Inkjet Printing</li><li>Electron Beam Melting (EBM)</li><li>Laser Metal Deposition (LMD)</li><li>Direct Light Projection (DLP)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>In the industrial market, 3D printing encompasses various technologies. Stereolithography (SLA) uses a laser to solidify liquid resin, while Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) extrudes melted filament layer by layer. Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) sinters powdered material with a laser, and Direct Metal Laser Sintering (DMLS) does the same with metal. PolyJet Printing (MJP) jets photopolymer materials, Inkjet Printing deposits layers of liquid binder onto powder, and Electron Beam Melting (EBM) uses an electron beam to melt powdered metal. Laser Metal Deposition (LMD) adds material in layers, and Direct Light Projection (DLP) solidifies liquid resin with a projector.</p></p>
