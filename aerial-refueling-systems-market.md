<p><p>Aerial refueling systems are essential for extending the flight range and endurance of military aircraft. There are two main types of systems: probe and drogue and boom and receptacle. In the probe and drogue system, a hose with a drogue attached to its end is extended from a tanker aircraft. The receiver aircraft has a probe that accurately inserts into the drogue, allowing for fuel transfer. In contrast, the boom and receptacle system involves a rigid "boom" extending from the tanker aircraft, and the receiver aircraft has a receptacle that engages with the boom for fuel transfer.</p></p>
