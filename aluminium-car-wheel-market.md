<p><p>The aluminium car wheel market includes several types of manufacturing processes. Casting is a common method where molten aluminium is poured into a mold to create the wheel shape. Forging involves shaping the wheel by applying heat and pressure to a solid aluminium block. Other methods may include spinning, where a disc of aluminium is formed into a wheel shape, or machining, where a wheel is carved out of a solid billet of aluminium. These different manufacturing processes offer various benefits in terms of cost, strength, weight, and design flexibility.</p></p>
