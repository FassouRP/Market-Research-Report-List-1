<p><p>The canned beef market can be categorized into two major types: fresh and frozen. Fresh canned beef refers to meat that is processed and canned soon after slaughter, without undergoing freezing. This type of canned beef has a shorter shelf life and requires refrigeration. On the other hand, frozen canned beef is made from meat that is first frozen and then processed and canned. This type of canned beef has a longer shelf life and does not require refrigeration until opened. Both fresh and frozen options provide convenient and preserved meat products for consumers.</p></p>
