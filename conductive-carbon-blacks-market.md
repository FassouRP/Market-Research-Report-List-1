<p><p>Conductive Carbon Blacks (CB) have different types based on their grades, namely CC, CF, SCF, and XCF. CC refers to the carbon black powder processed by furnace, while CF represents carbon black particles with conductive properties that are obtained through a gas phase process. SCF stands for super conductivity carbon black, which is achieved by surface modification on CF types. Lastly, XCF is the form of carbon black with even better electric conductivity compared to SCF, as it is produced by a special treatment process.</p></p>
