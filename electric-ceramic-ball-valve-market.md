Low Temperature Valves are designed to withstand extremely cold conditions, typically below -40 degrees Celsius. Normal Temperature Valves are suitable for regular temperature ranges, typically ranging from -20 to 200 degrees Celsius. High Temperature Valves are specifically engineered to operate in high-temperature environments, typically above 200 degrees Celsius. These distinctions allow users to select the appropriate valve based on their specific temperature needs.</p></p>
