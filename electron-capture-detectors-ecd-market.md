<p><p>Electron Capture Detectors (ECD) are analytical devices used to detect and measure trace amounts of specific gases and compounds. There are two main types: Radioactive ECDs use a radioactive source to produce electrons, which are captured by the analyte molecules. Non-radioactive ECDs use a non-radioactive source, such as a radioactive metal-free porous material, to generate electrons. Both variants operate on the principle of electron capture by analyte molecules, resulting in a detectable signal that can be used for analysis and quantification purposes.</p></p>
