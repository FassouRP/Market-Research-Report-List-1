<p><p>Electrophoretic coating is a process used to apply a protective and decorative coating on various materials. Anodic electrophoretic coating involves oxidizing the surface to create a protective layer, while cathodic electrophoretic coating uses a negatively charged paint that is attracted to the positively charged surface. The anodic coating is suitable for non-conductive materials, while the cathodic coating is used for conductive materials. Both types offer excellent corrosion resistance, even coating thickness, and high efficiency, making them widely used in industries such as automotive, appliances, and electronics.</p></p>
