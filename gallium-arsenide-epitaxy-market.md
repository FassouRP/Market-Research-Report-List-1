<p><p>The gallium arsenide epitaxy market is segmented based on various deposition techniques. Metalorganic Chemical Vapor Deposition (MOCVD), Molecular Beam Epitaxy (MBE), and Liquid Phase Epitaxy (LPE) are the primary methods used for gallium arsenide fabrication. MOCVD involves the deposition of thin layers using chemical reactions, while MBE grows layers atom by atom with the help of high-energy beams. LPE utilizes liquid solutions enriched with gallium and arsenic for layer formation. Additionally, there are other techniques developed for gallium arsenide epitaxy, but these three are the most widely used in the industry.</p></p>
