<p><p>The gas bearing market can be classified into two types: aerostatic and aerodynamic. In an aerostatic gas bearing, the separation of the surfaces is achieved by pressurizing the gas film between the bearing surfaces. There is no contact between the surfaces, resulting in low friction and wear. On the other hand, in an aerodynamic gas bearing, the gas film is created by the relative motion between the surfaces, providing a dynamic lift. This type of bearing is suitable for high-speed applications but may have higher friction and wear compared to aerostatic bearings.</p></p>
