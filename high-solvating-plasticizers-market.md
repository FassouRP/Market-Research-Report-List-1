<p><p>High solvating plasticizers are chemical additives that are used to improve the flexibility and durability of various polymer materials. They can be classified into two main types: phthalate and non-phthalate plasticizers. Phthalate plasticizers, such as diethylhexyl phthalate (DEHP) and dibutyl phthalate (DBP), have been traditionally used due to their low cost and versatile properties. However, concerns regarding their potential negative health and environmental impacts have led to the development and increasing adoption of non-phthalate plasticizers. These alternatives, including adipates, benzoates, and citrates, offer similar performance characteristics while meeting stricter regulations and consumer demand for safer and more sustainable products.</p></p>
