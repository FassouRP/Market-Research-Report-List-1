<p><p>The Impact-Modified Nylon market consists of two types: Nylon 6 and Nylon 66. Nylon 6 is a thermoplastic material made by polymerizing caprolactam. It possesses excellent mechanical properties, high tensile strength, and heat resistance. Nylon 66, on the other hand, is also a thermoplastic material but is manufactured by combining adipic acid and hexamethylenediamine. It offers high thermal stability, abrasion resistance, and durability. Both types of Impact-Modified Nylon find extensive applications in industries such as automotive, electronics, consumer goods, and packaging due to their superior properties and versatility.</p></p>
