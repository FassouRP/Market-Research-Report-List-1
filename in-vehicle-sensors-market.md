<p><p>In-vehicle sensors market consists of two types: wired and wireless sensors. The wired sensors are connected using physical cables and require direct connection to the vehicle's system. They provide reliable data transmission, but their installation can be complex and time-consuming. On the other hand, wireless sensors use radio frequency technology to transmit data to the vehicle's system without the need for physical cables. They offer easier and quicker installation but may be prone to signal interference. Both types play a vital role in gathering and transmitting information for various vehicle applications.</p></p>
