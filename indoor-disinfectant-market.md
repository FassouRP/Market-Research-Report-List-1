<p><p>The indoor disinfectant market includes two main types: non-oxidizing disinfectants and oxidizing disinfectants. Non-oxidizing disinfectants are substances that work by disrupting the cell walls of microorganisms, preventing their growth and reproduction. These disinfectants are commonly used on surfaces and objects in households, healthcare settings, and industries to maintain cleanliness and prevent the spread of infections. On the other hand, oxidizing disinfectants work by releasing oxygen molecules that attack and destroy microorganisms. This type of disinfectant is typically used in settings where more aggressive germ-killing properties are required, such as water treatment facilities and sewage systems.</p></p>
