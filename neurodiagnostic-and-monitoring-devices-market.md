<p><p>Neurodiagnostic and monitoring devices refer to various medical tools used in the assessment and monitoring of neurological conditions. Electroencephalogram (EEG) records electrical activity in the brain, while electromyography (EMG) measures muscle activity. Transcranial Doppler evaluates blood flow velocity in the brain, and intracranial pressure monitors measure pressure inside the skull. Cerebral oximeters monitor oxygen levels in brain tissue. These devices play crucial roles in diagnosing neurological disorders, monitoring treatment effectiveness, and ensuring patient safety during surgeries or critical care.</p></p>
