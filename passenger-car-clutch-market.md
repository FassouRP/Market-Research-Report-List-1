<p><p>In the passenger car clutch market, there are two prominent types of clutches available: friction clutches and electromagnetic clutches. </p><p>Friction clutches operate by using frictional forces between a clutch disc and a flywheel to transmit power from the engine to the transmission. They have been widely used in passenger cars for their efficiency and reliability.</p><p>On the other hand, electromagnetic clutches utilize electromagnetic fields to engage and disengage the power transmission. These clutches offer benefits such as rapid response times, precise control, and reduced wear and tear, making them suitable for various applications in passenger cars.</p></p>
