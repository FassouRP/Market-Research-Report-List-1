<p><p>The plant derived sugar market consists of natural sugars obtained from plants. Arabinose, fructose, fucose, glucose, xylose, galactose, rhamnose, and mannose are different types of plant derived sugars. Arabinose is derived from sugar beet pulp and corn fiber, while fructose is found in fruits and honey. Fucose is obtained from seaweed, glucose is found in various plants, and xylose can be derived from corn cobs or hardwoods. Galactose is obtained from dairy products, rhamnose is found in buckthorn berries, and mannose can be derived from plants like aloe vera. These sugars are widely used in various industries including food and beverages, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics. </p><p>On the other hand, synthetic sugars are artificially produced through chemical processes. These sugars are created to mimic natural sugars but are not obtained from plants. Synthetic sugars are typically made from substances like corn or wheat starch using enzymatic or chemical reactions. They are commonly used as sweeteners in food and beverage products as an alternative to natural sugars. However, due to concerns about their potential health effects, synthetic sugars are often subject to strict regulations and scrutiny.</p></p>
