<p><p>Rosin, also known as colophony, is a natural resin extracted from coniferous trees. It is used in various industries, including adhesives, coatings, cosmetics, and food. The market usually categorizes rosin into three types: gum rosin, tall oil rosin, and wood rosin. Gum rosin is obtained from the sap of pine trees, while tall oil rosin is a byproduct of the paper industry. Wood rosin is derived from the destructive distillation of wood. Each type has distinct characteristics and applications, catering to different industries' needs.</p></p>
