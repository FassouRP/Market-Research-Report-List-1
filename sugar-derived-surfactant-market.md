<p><p>The sugar derived surfactant market consists of several types, including alkyl polyglycosides (APGs), decyl glucoside, and sucrose cocoate. APGs are non-ionic surfactants made from the reaction between sugar and fatty alcohols. They are often used in personal care products due to their mildness and biodegradability. Decyl glucoside is another non-ionic surfactant derived from glucose and fatty alcohols. It is known for its gentle cleansing and foam boosting properties, commonly employed in skincare and hair care products. Sucrose cocoate is an ester derived from sugar and coconut oil. It acts as an emollient and moisturizer in various cosmetics and personal care items.</p></p>
