<p><p>Titanium dioxide (TiO2) is a versatile white pigment used in various industries. The market is divided into two types: sulfate process and chloride process. The sulfate process involves treating titanium ore with sulfuric acid, resulting in a higher production cost but a more sustainable and environmentally friendly end product. In contrast, the chloride process uses chlorine to produce TiO2, leading to lower production costs but also higher environmental impact. Both processes have their advantages and disadvantages, with the choice depending on factors such as cost, efficiency, and environmental considerations.</p></p>
