<p><p>Torrefied pellets are a type of biofuel made from wood biomass that has been subjected to torrefaction, a thermal treatment process. During torrefaction, the biomass is heated in the absence of oxygen, resulting in the release of volatile components such as water, hemicellulose, and other low-energy components. The end product is a dry, high-density, and energy-rich pellet that can be used as a substitute for fossil fuels in various industries, such as power generation and heating.</p><p>The future outlook for the torrefied pellets market is promising. <p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The torrefied pellets market includes two main types: torrefaction and steam explosion (SE). Torrefaction involves heating biomass materials in a low-oxygen environment to remove moisture and volatile compounds, resulting in a highly porous and energy-dense pellet. Steam explosion, on the other hand, involves rapidly heating moist biomass and then rapidly depressurizing it, causing the materials to explode and break down into smaller particles that can be pelletized. Both torrefaction and steam explosion processes aim to enhance the energy density and reduce the moisture content of biomass pellets, making them more efficient for various applications.</p></p>
