<p><p>The train disc brake linings market is comprised of three types: powder metallurgy, cast iron, and composite. Powder metallurgy linings are made by compacting metal powders under high pressure and then heating them. Cast iron linings are manufactured by melting iron and pouring it into molds to form the desired shape. Composite linings are made by combining different materials, such as organic materials or aramid fibers, with resins or other binders. These different types of linings offer varying levels of performance and durability for train disc brakes.</p></p>
