<p><p>Wire compound and cable compound are materials used for insulation and jacketing purposes in the electrical and telecommunications industry. Halogenated polymers are compounds containing elements such as chlorine, fluorine, bromine, etc., which provide flame retardant properties. Non-halogenated polymers, on the other hand, do not contain these elements and therefore may have lower flame retardancy. Both types of compounds serve the same purpose of protecting wires and cables from heat, moisture, mechanical stress, and other environmental factors, but differ in their flame retardant properties.</p></p>
